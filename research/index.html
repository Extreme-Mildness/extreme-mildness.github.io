Working papers

Squeezing More Juice Out of Lime: Optimal Pricing in Shared Vehicle Platforms (2023).

Abstract: Price controls that seek to balance economic eﬀiciency with redistribution between different groups of consumers have a long history of use in transporta- tion policy. The emergence of modern platforms for transportation have re- newed discussions of regulating prices, though little evidence exists on whether they accomplish the goals of progressive redistribution. I study the market for shared electric vehicle platforms in Washington D.C., where prices are man- dated to be uniform across origin and destination. I first estimate a demand system using high-frequency geolocation data from all firms in the market. Using a novel reinforcement learning algorithm, I then compute counterfactual equilibria in which platforms can freely set a sophisticated schedule of hundreds of origin and destination specific prices. Comparing these to prices constrained by the regulation, I find that the redistributive effects of the price controls are mild, and mainly serve riders in the periphery of the city, which does not correlate directly with income. Furthermore, I find that relaxing the regulation increases rides taken by consumers by 41%, firm profits by 34%, and increases consumer welfare by more than double the profit increase (80% of firm profits).
